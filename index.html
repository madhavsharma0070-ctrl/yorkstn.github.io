What We Do

The Problems
We Solve for You

India's premium retail market is full of opportunity, and full of friction. Yorkstn removes the friction so brands stay focused on what they do best.

Why brands struggle
in India, and how we fix it

Every problem below is real and recurring. We've built Yorkstn's model around solving each one before they become your problem.

01

Regulatory Complexity

BIS certifications, CAROTAR customs rules, GST registration, DPIIT approvals — India's compliance stack is layered and unforgiving for foreign brands.

We manage the complete regulatory roadmap before your first shipment arrives in India.

02

Wrong Retail Location

Premium real estate in Delhi NCR requires local relationships and negotiation fluency. International brands often overpay or sign unfavourable lease terms.

We identify, evaluate, and negotiate retail spaces that align precisely to your brand tier.

03

Store Identity Gets Lost

Indian buildout vendors rarely understand artisanal brand aesthetics. Material compromises misrepresent your brand at its most critical first moment.

We project-manage every detail of the store setup against your brand guidelines, end to end.

04

No Ground-Level Intelligence

Generic market reports cannot tell you whether your specific category and price point will resonate with the premium consumer segment in Gurugram.

We deliver a tailored India feasibility report built specifically around your brand and category.

05

Supply Chain Complexity

Freight forwarding, customs clearance, GST-compliant invoicing, e-way bills — without a local expert this leads to stockouts, customs holds, and margin erosion.

We operate your full India-side supply chain, from your origin country to the shelf in Gurugram.

06

Speed Dilutes Craft

Scaling too fast across multiple cities with a wide SKU range is the most common reason once-aspirational brands become commonplace within a few years.

We build genuine community first. Geography and scale follow only once the foundation is real.

INDIA
India Market

The Opportunity
is Right Now

India is set to become the world's third-largest retail market by 2030. Its premium consumer base is shifting toward quality, provenance, and long-term value. Delhi NCR is where this shift is most concentrated.

Why India, Why Now

India doesn't need
more brands.
It needs the right ones.

India's retail market reached USD 993 billion in 2024 and is projected to grow at a 13% CAGR through 2033, according to IMARC Group. Simultaneously, the premiumisation trend is structural: India's affluent class now comprises around 12 million households, with North India holding over 35% of the national luxury goods market share, the largest of any region.

The artisanal brand consumer in India is well-travelled, globally informed, and actively seeking products with genuine provenance and long-term value. The challenge isn't demand. It's the absence of a disciplined entry partner who protects brand identity while navigating India's structural complexity. That gap is exactly what Yorkstn fills.

Why Artisanal, Why Now

A shared philosophy
of craft & patience

Artisanal brand-building is rooted in discipline, patience, and deep respect for craft. This philosophy connects meaningfully with India's premium consumers, who are increasingly seeking products with authentic provenance and a story behind them.

According to a Deloitte-FICCI report, premiumisation is now one of the four key growth drivers reshaping Indian retail. More than 60% of Gen Z and millennial consumers prefer brands with transparent, authentic practices. The cultural fit for craft-led brands entering India has never been stronger.

I

Philosophical Alignment

Artisanal brands build for longevity. India's premium consumer increasingly does the same. The connection is cultural, not just commercial.

II

Category White Space

Global craft categories, including ceramics, stationery, lifestyle goods, and artisanal fashion, have very limited authentic representation in India's premium retail landscape.

III

First-Mover Advantage

Artisanal brands that establish presence in India now will define what global craft means to the Indian consumer for years ahead. That window is narrowing.
